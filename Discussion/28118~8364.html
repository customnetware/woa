Update on CenterPoint Container Yard Project Following 6-17-19 Meeting
Total Replies:
0
Last Reply:
6/17/19 9:34 PM

First, thank you to everyone who attended.  The meeting was not actually planned to be an open forum as such, but reflecting back on this evening, it was a definite benefit to start the meeting with public Q&A.  I think Gary Singh and Jose Nuno were both impressed with the strong concerns Woodbridge has regarding this project.  Now, going forward:

1. This operating hours for the Container Yard have been appealed by CenterPoint to the City Council.  So when we do get to say our concerns, it will actually be for an item on the agenda.  Yea!

2. The appeal is not on tomorrow night's (6/18/19) City Council agenda.

3. Both Gary and Jose have advised us not to attend tomorrow night's City Council meeting for the purpose of speaking about the CenterPoint project.  We don't want to rock the boat at this point because CenterPoint could choose to withdraw their Appeal if we make too much of a fuss at this time.  If you have another reason to attend the meeting, by all means do so.

4. The next City Council meeting after tomorrow night will be July 16th as July 2nd is too close to the 4th of July.  The 16th will be the only City Council meeting in July.

5. The appeal may or may not be placed on the July 16th agenda.  The City Council will have a lot of heavy items on that agenda: labor negotiations, approval of annual budget, etc.

6. However, we will be prepared for July 16th, or any subsequent Council meeting!

7. As of tonight, we broke the 1,000 signature mark.  We are to continue getting signatures as 2,000 is even better than 1,000.  If the signature gatherers need more blank petitions, please let me know.

The strategy going forward is going to be to emphasize what the best solution is--completion of Intermodal Way (the Spine Rd) and using the petition points to underpin that argument as in:  there would be less degradation of air quality if the trucks stayed internal on Intermodal Way without having to stop a couple times on Airport Way which is closer to our residential area.

We are also going to try to entertain more of the City Council members to a similar meeting as we had tonight and we'll use much the same format as we ended up with this evening.  It worked out well!  Thanks again to everyone attending including our special guests, Gary and Jose.

Bill Barnhart
